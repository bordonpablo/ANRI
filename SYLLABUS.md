# Syllabus — Advanced Geophysical Methods in Natural Resource Investigation (ANRI)

**Module:** 14506 | **Credits:** 6 ECTS | **Language:** English
**Programme:** Master Environmental and Resource Management (PO 2021)
**Responsible:** Dr. Pablo Bordón 

---

## Prerequisites

- Basic knowledge of physics and mathematics at BSc level
- Fundamentals of geology and earth sciences
- Basic GIS skills recommended

---

## Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

**Knowledge**
- Describe the physical principles underlying the main near-surface and airborne geophysical methods
- Explain the scope, limitations, and appropriate application contexts of each method
- Identify suitable geophysical approaches for given resource investigation problems

**Skills**
- Plan and conduct geophysical field surveys
- Process and interpret geophysical datasets using standard software tools
- Integrate multi-method results in a GIS environment
- Critically evaluate data quality and sources of uncertainty

**Competences**
- Select and justify geophysical methods for specific environmental and resource management tasks
- Communicate results clearly in written reports and oral presentations
- Work effectively in small field teams

## Course Contents

### Unit 01 — Introduction to Applied Geophysics
Overview of the course scope and objectives. Classification of geophysical prospection methods. Physical properties of earth materials. Overview of applications in natural resource investigation. Survey design principles.

### Unit 02 — Ground Penetrating Radar (GPR)
Electromagnetic wave propagation in the subsurface. Antenna frequency selection. Field acquisition strategies. Reflection profiling and common-midpoint surveys. Data processing: zero-time correction, filtering, migration. Interpretation of radargrams. Applications in archaeology, engineering, and environmental studies.

### Unit 03 — GIS Fundamentals
Coordinate reference systems and map projections. Vector and raster data models. Spatial data management. Integration of geophysical survey data in GIS. Basic geostatistics and interpolation. Visualization and map production.

### Unit 04 — Magnetometry
Earth's magnetic field and its anomalies. Instrumentation: fluxgate, proton precession, and cesium vapour magnetometers. Field acquisition: profiles and grids. Data corrections (diurnal variation, IGRF). Processing and filtering techniques. Interpretation of magnetic anomalies. Applications in mineral exploration and environmental investigations.

### Unit 05 — Electrical Resistivity Tomography (ERT)
Electrical properties of rocks and soils. DC resistivity principles. Electrode configurations (Wenner, Schlumberger, dipole-dipole). Field data acquisition with multi-electrode systems. Inversion theory and software (e.g., RES2DINV). 2D and 3D ERT. Applications in groundwater, contamination mapping, and geotechnical investigations.

### Unit 06 — Airborne Magnetics and Radiometrics
Principles of airborne survey platforms (fixed-wing, helicopter, UAV). Aeromagnetic data acquisition and quality control. Radiometric methods: gamma-ray spectrometry, K/U/Th mapping. Data processing and levelling. Geological and structural interpretation. Applications in mineral and hydrocarbon exploration.
